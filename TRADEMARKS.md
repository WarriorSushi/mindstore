# MindStore Trademark Policy

This policy covers the project name, logos, word marks, and branding associated with MindStore.

Code licenses and trademark rights are separate.

## Reserved Marks

The following are reserved by the project maintainers unless explicitly licensed:

- `MindStore`
- official MindStore logos
- official brand graphics, badges, and wordmarks

## Allowed Uses

You may generally use the project name in descriptive, factual ways such as:

- saying your plugin works with MindStore
- linking to the MindStore repository
- writing tutorials, reviews, benchmarks, or comparisons
- discussing compatibility with MindStore APIs, MCP surfaces, or plugin interfaces

Examples of generally acceptable wording:

- `Compatible with MindStore`
- `Plugin for MindStore`
- `Works with MindStore MCP`

## Not Allowed Without Permission

You may not, without explicit permission:

- imply your fork, service, plugin, or package is the official MindStore project
- use the MindStore logo as the primary branding for your own product
- name a hosted service or fork in a way that is likely to confuse users into thinking it is the official one
- use MindStore branding in paid ads, marketplace listings, or product packaging in a misleading way

## Forks

Forks are welcome.

If you distribute a fork publicly, the safest practice is:

- use your own project name
- make it clear that your version is an unofficial fork
- avoid using official MindStore logos

## Plugins

Plugin authors are encouraged to say their work is compatible with MindStore, but should not brand a plugin in a way that suggests official endorsement unless that endorsement has actually been granted.

## Questions

If you are unsure whether a use is acceptable, ask the maintainers before launch rather than after.
